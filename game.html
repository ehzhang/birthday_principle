What is the Birthday Principle?

If there are 25 people in a room, what is the probability that 2 people share a birthday?

Let's start smaller (or larger!). What if there were 366 people in the room?

This comes from the Pigeonhole Principle: "If there are more pigeons than holes, at least two pigeons must be in the same hole." In our example, there were 366 pigeons and 365 holes that the pigeons could fit in to. So two of them had to go into the same hole!
